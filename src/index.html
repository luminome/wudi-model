Intense wind events force horizontal and vertical water movements near the coast

Intermittent wind-driven coastal upwelling and downwelling are ubiquitous processes that drive a large part of the high frequency variability of coastal hydrography, with potential implications for ecosystems and socio-economic activities.

The upwelling of deep, cold and nutrient rich waters to the surface (resp. the downwelling of surface waters to the seabed) are found to occur sporadically along specific Mediterranean coastlines and last for days to weeks.

Analyzing a long-term archive of Sea Surface Winds from ERA5 reanalysis together with high-resolution bathymetry from EMODnet, we calculate a Wind-based Upwelling and Downwelling Index (WUDI, Odic et al. 2022) at daily resolution along Mediterranean shorelines.

A climatological database of wind-driven events was constructed for the 1979-2020 period, allowing the calculation of robust statistics on their annual and seasonal occurrence along the various shoreline portions. Here you can explore 42 years of statistics and access to WUDI intensity (cross-shore Ekman transport) for any event.

Credits and Support

These results are parts of the action “Developing Downstream applications and services on BIO-PHYsical characterization of the seascape for COASTal management” (BIOPHYCOAST) funded by the European Commission through the program “Caroline Herschel” for Copernicus user uptake.

WUDI calculations are based on:

• Hourly wind data at 10m height and 20 km spatial resolution from the ERA5 reanalysis available from the Copernicus Climate Data Store.

• EMODnet bathymetry at high resolution (115 m) available here considering the 20 m isobath obtained by interpolation of the original data.

• Marine Protected Areas information were obtained from the MAPAMED data base 2021 (https://www.mapamed.org/)

• Population centers geolocation data from OpenStreetMap.org (https://openstreetmap.org/copyright)

• Population centers supplemental data from Wikidata.org (https://www.wikidata.org)

For more information review: Odic M, Bensoussan N., Pinazo C., Taupier-Letage I., Rossi V. (2022). Sporadic Wind-Driven Upwelling/Downwelling and Associated Cooling/Warming along the North-West Mediterranean Coastlines. Continental Shelf Research. In press.
